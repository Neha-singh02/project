The float property in CSS allows you to take an element out of the normal document flow and position it to the left or right of its container. This enables surrounding content, such as text, to wrap around the floated element, creating a more dynamic layout. The float property can take the following values: left: The element floats to the left of its container, allowing content to flow around its right side. right: The element floats to the right of its container, allowing content to flow around its left side. none: The default value; the element does not float and will be displayed just where it occurs in the text. inherit: The element inherits the float value from its parent element.
